# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

`auto-seed` should be a welcoming, practical place to collaborate. Everyone who participates in the project is expected to act with respect, assume good intent, and keep discussion focused on improving the software and its documentation.

This code applies to all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, commit messages, and any other public or private communication related to the project.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful of differing experience levels, viewpoints, and constraints.
- Give and receive feedback constructively.
- Keep technical disagreement specific, evidence-based, and focused on the work.
- Use inclusive language and avoid personal attacks.
- Respect people's time by keeping reports, reviews, and requests clear and actionable.
- Credit others for ideas, reports, and contributions where appropriate.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable in this project:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or sustained disruption.
- Insults, personal attacks, trolling, or deliberate inflammatory comments.
- Discriminatory language or conduct based on identity, background, or personal characteristics.
- Publishing someone else's private information without clear permission.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted sexual attention.
- Repeatedly ignoring maintainer requests to stop a behavior.
- Any conduct that would reasonably make participation unsafe or hostile.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code, report it privately by emailing **seif.kh021@gmail.com**.

Please include:

- A short description of what happened.
- Links, screenshots, or other context when available.
- The names or handles of the people involved, if known.
- Whether you are comfortable being contacted for follow-up.

Reports will be handled as confidentially as possible. Maintainers will review the report, ask for more information if needed, and take action appropriate to the situation.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they consider necessary to protect the project community, including:

- A private warning or clarification.
- Editing, hiding, or deleting comments.
- Closing or locking issues or pull requests.
- Temporary or permanent bans from project spaces.
- Reporting severe incidents to the relevant platform or authority.

Enforcement decisions should be proportionate to the impact of the behavior, the available context, and whether the behavior is repeated after a warning.

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards, applying this code consistently, and correcting behavior that undermines a respectful project environment. Maintainers are also expected to follow this code themselves.
